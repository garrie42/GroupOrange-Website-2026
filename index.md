## Project Overview

Healthcare organizations still struggle with fragmented communication, delayed referrals, and inefficient record exchange.
PatientPath addresses these challenges through a secure, web-based system that enables providers to share records digitally,
track transfer status, and support interoperability across different healthcare environments.

Rather than functioning as a full electronic health record system, PatientPath focuses on one critical gap:
**secure, efficient cross-organization exchange of patient information**.

## Problem Statement

Medical record transfer methods are often inefficient, disconnected, and dependent on outdated communication tools.
When providers rely on fax-based systems, records may be delayed, incomplete, unreadable, or lost entirely.
These breakdowns can slow referrals, postpone treatment, increase duplicate testing, and create frustration for both
providers and patients.

PatientPath is built around the idea that patients should not be forced to carry the burden of fragmented healthcare communication.

## Solution Statement

PatientPath is a secure, centralized digital platform that enables healthcare providers to upload, access, manage,
and share patient records without relying on fax machines. It is designed to act as a bridge between organizations,
supporting efficient communication while improving visibility, accountability, and security throughout the transfer process.

The platform emphasizes practical healthcare workflow improvement rather than replacing every existing system.
Its purpose is to modernize one of the most persistent pain points in healthcare communication: moving patient information quickly and safely.

## Project Vision

PatientPath reflects a simple but important idea: patient information should move with the same urgency as patient care.
By replacing slow, manual communication with secure digital exchange, PatientPath aims to make healthcare coordination more
efficient, more reliable, and more patient-centered.
